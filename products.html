Tectona grandis Teak wood

Teak is one of the most valuable tropical hardwoods in the world, highly prized for its natural strength, durability, and timeless beauty. Its unique properties make it resistant to moisture, insects, and decay, which is why teak has been used for centuries in shipbuilding, outdoor furniture, and high-end construction projects. The golden-brown color combined with elegant grain patterns gives teak an unmistakable appearance that matures beautifully over time, developing a rich patina. Thanks to its stability and resistance to weather conditions, teak is an ideal material for flooring, decking, luxury furniture, and architectural applications where both aesthetics and durability are essential.

Swietenia macrophylla Mahagony

Mahogany is a classic exotic hardwood renowned for its luxurious appearance and exceptional workability. Its rich reddish-brown tones and smooth, straight grain make it a favorite choice for premium furniture, decorative interiors, and fine craftsmanship. Beyond its visual appeal, mahogany is strong, stable, and resistant to shrinking or warping, ensuring long-lasting performance even under demanding conditions. It is widely used in the production of elegant furniture, musical instruments, cabinetry, and interior paneling, where its warm color and natural luster enhance the atmosphere of any space. Mahogany is not only a symbol of elegance but also a guarantee of quality that has stood the test of time in the most prestigious applications.
